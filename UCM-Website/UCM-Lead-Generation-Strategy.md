## RESIDENTIAL LEAD SOURCES

### **FREE/LOW-COST SOURCES**

**1. Public Records & Directories**
- **Voter Registration Lists** (public records)
  - Cost: $50-200 per county
  - Data: Names, addresses, phone numbers, age
  - Target: Ages 35-65 (highest giving potential)

- **Property Tax Records** (public)
  - Cost: Free-$100 per county
  - Data: Homeowner names, addresses, property values
  - Target: Mid-to-high value properties

- **Business License Directories** (public)
  - Cost: Free-$50 per city
  - Data: Business owner names and contact info
  - Target: Local small business owners

**2. Online Directories**
- **WhitePages.com** - Basic contact information
- **Spokeo.com** - Enhanced demographic data
- **BeenVerified.com** - Comprehensive background data
- **TruePeopleSearch.com** - Free basic information

**3. Social Media Mining**
- **Facebook Local Groups** - Community-minded individuals
- **Nextdoor App** - Neighborhood-focused residents
- **LinkedIn Local** - Professional community members
- **Church Facebook Pages** - Faith-based prospects

### **PAID LEAD SOURCES**

**1. Data Brokers (RECOMMENDED)**

**InfoUSA/Data.com:**
- **Cost:** $0.10-0.30 per record
- **Data Quality:** High accuracy, updated monthly

- **Volume:** 10,000+ records available per area

**Experian/Acxiom:**
- **Cost:** $0.15-0.40 per record

**ZoomInfo (Business + Personal):**

- **Volume:** 50,000+ records per market

**2. Nonprofit-Specific Lists**

**3. Lead Generation Services**

**LeadGenius:**
- **Cost:** $1-3 per qualified lead
- **Service:** Custom research and verification
- **Quality:** Hand-verified contact information
- **Volume:** 1,000-5,000 per month

**UpLead:**

## COMPLIANCE & LEGAL

### **DO NOT CALL REGISTRY**

**Requirements:**
- Scrub all lists against DNC registry monthly
- Maintain internal opt-out list
- Honor removal requests immediately
- Document compliance procedures

**Nonprofit Exemptions:**
- Can call DNC registered numbers for donations
- Must identify as nonprofit organization
- Must provide opt-out mechanism
- Cannot use automated dialers without consent

### **DATA PRIVACY**

**CCPA Compliance (California):**
- Disclose data collection practices
- Honor deletion requests
- Secure data storage requirements
- Third-party data sharing limitations

**General Best Practices:**
- Encrypt all lead data
- Limit access to authorized personnel
- Regular data backup and security audits
- Clear data retention policies
