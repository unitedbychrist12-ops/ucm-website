Our Mission

"At United by Christ Ministries, our mission is to reduce homelessness and empower individuals to achieve holistic well-being by providing comprehensive 1, 2, 3, and 4 step programs that address mental, physical, financial, and spiritual needs. Through compassionate support and guidance, we aim to restore hope and dignity, equipping those we serve to rejoin society as productive and fulfilled citizens, united by their faith and renewed purpose."

Our Story

United by Christ Ministries is dedicated to reach out to the Alcoholic, Drug Addict, Gang Member, Felon, Incarcerated, Homeless Adults & Families and any Souls in need of God's Love, Grace and Mercy.

Founded in 2018 by Aureliano "Audie" Reyes, UCM was born from personal experiences of restoration and renewal. Our founder once walked the difficult path of addiction and hardships, finding hope through ministries like Barrios Unidos En Cristo and other Church Parishes in California with similar Ministry groups.

Founder's Story

"I once found myself lost, searching desperately for answers and needing a drastic change in my life. My journey through addiction, gang involvement, and the streets had left me broken and without direction. Through this journey, I discovered that true recovery requires more than just sobriety‚Äîit demands holistic restoration of mind, body, spirit, and purpose.

On April 13, 2018, United by Christ Ministries was born‚Äînot just as an organization, but as a fulfillment of that sacred promise I made to God. Program Phases & Timeline

Phase 1: Foundation (Steps 1-2)

Duration: 90-120 days

Residential or intensive outpatient with daily programming, crisis stabilization, and medical/mental health foundation.

Phase 2: Development (Steps 2-3)

Duration: 6-12 months

Transitional housing, skill building emphasis, vocational training, and community integration preparation.

Phase 3: Independence (Steps 3-4)

Duration: 12-24 months

Independent living support, career advancement, spiritual leadership development, and community service.

Phase 4: Mentorship (Step 4+)

Duration: Ongoing

Alumni program participation, peer mentoring roles, community leadership, and continued spiritual growth.

About Our Programs

What makes UCM's 4-step program different from other recovery programs?

+

UCM's program is unique because it addresses the complete person - mental, physical, financial, and spiritual needs - through licensed professionals in each domain. Unlike programs that focus on just one aspect, we provide comprehensive, evidence-based treatment with measurable outcomes while maintaining a strong faith foundation.

How long does the program take?

+

The program is designed in phases: Foundation (90-120 days), Development (6-12 months), Independence (12-24 months), and ongoing Mentorship. However, everyone progresses at their own pace based on individual needs and circumstances. Case managers provide support for up to 5 years.

Do I have to be Christian to participate?

+

No. While UCM is a faith-based organization, we welcome people of all backgrounds and beliefs. Our spiritual component respects individual faith journeys and focuses on purpose, meaning, and community connection. You're never required to adopt specific religious beliefs.

What if I've tried other programs before and failed?

+

Previous attempts are not failures - they're learning experiences. Our holistic approach addresses underlying issues that may not have been treated before. Many of our most successful participants had multiple previous attempts. We believe in second, third, and fourth chances.

Getting Started

How do I get into the program?

+

Start with our basic intake assessment - either through street outreach, walk-in, or by calling (951) 992-2068. We'll connect you with immediate needs first, then schedule a comprehensive professional evaluation if you're interested in our full program.

What does the program cost?

+

UCM's services are provided at no cost to participants. We're funded through donations, grants, and our innovative service-switching program through R HOME app. Your only "payment" is commitment to your recovery and helping others when you're able.

Do I need insurance or ID to get help?

+

No - we'll help you regardless of insurance or documentation status. In fact, helping you obtain ID, insurance, and benefits is part of our program. We believe everyone deserves help, and paperwork shouldn't be a barrier to getting your life back.

What if I'm not ready for a full program but need help now?

+

We meet you where you are. Through our 12 ministries, we provide immediate assistance with food, clothing, shelter referrals, and crisis support. There's no pressure to commit to the full program until you're ready.

Family & Support

Can my family be involved in my recovery?

+

Absolutely! Our UC Family Formation ministry works to rebuild and strengthen family relationships. We provide family counseling, education, and support groups. Healthy family connections are often crucial for long-term success.

What if I have children? Can they be with me?

+

We work with families to develop safe, appropriate plans. This might include family housing options, supervised visits, or working toward reunification. Our UC Children Formation ministry provides support and services for kids affected by their parents' struggles.

How can my family support UCM's work?

+

Families can donate, volunteer, or support through our R HOME app service-switching program. Many families find that supporting UCM helps them process their own experiences and gives meaning to their struggles.

Professional Standards

Are your staff qualified and licensed?

+

Yes. All clinical assessments and treatment are provided by licensed professionals: MDs, RNs, Licensed Psychologists, LCSWs, Certified Financial Counselors, and Licensed Clinical Chaplains. We maintain the highest professional standards while keeping our faith-based approach.

How do you measure success?

+

We use evidence-based assessment tools and track measurable outcomes: sobriety rates, housing stability, employment success, health improvements, and community integration. We conduct 6-month, 1-year, 2-year, and 5-year follow-ups to ensure lasting change.

Is my information confidential?

+

Absolutely. We maintain strict HIPAA compliance and confidentiality protocols. Your information is only shared with your treatment team and only with your written consent. Your privacy and dignity are always protected.

Supporting UCM

How can I support UCM if I can't donate money?

+

Switch your services through our R HOME app! Get quotes for solar, internet, cell phones, TV, or merchant services. You get better rates, and 80% of our profits support UCM. It's a win-win way to help without extra cost to you.

What's the R HOME app and how does it help UCM?

+

R HOME is our innovative service-switching platform. Instead of traditional fundraising, we help people save money on services they already pay for (solar, internet, phones, etc.). 80% of our profits go directly to UCM's programs. It's sustainable funding that helps everyone.

Can I volunteer with UCM?

+

Yes! We need volunteers for street outreach, basic intake assessments, meal preparation, transportation, mentoring, and administrative support. Contact us to learn about volunteer training and opportunities that match your skills and availability.

Is UCM a legitimate 501(c)(3) nonprofit?

+

Yes. UCM is a federally recognized 501(c)(3) tax-exempt organization (EIN: 82-5038049) and California Entity #4130670. All donations are tax-deductible, and we maintain full financial transparency and accountability.
